Interview Guide

This table shows our questions for the initial interview stage. Together, these questions will provide us with a deeper understanding of how people cook in their homes, the factors leading to food wastage, how people order food, and they role technology plays in their kitchens.

Setting the right foundation
1. Context
  • What is the context you're focusing on?
  • Who is involved and why?
  • Why do you need to conduct an interview?
  • The food wastage in the household setting.
  • Individuals who cook and live busy lives. For practicality (1 week to source participants and unable to provide any substantial reward), our interviewees will be students.
  • We need to understand more deeply the causes of food waste in the home
2. Outcomes
  • What is the desired outcome of this interview?
  • What would you like to learn or validate?
  • Confirm that people waste food at home
  • Find out the reasons for household food wastage.
  • We propose that most food wastage in the home is a result of raw ingredients going bad.
3. Location
  • Where would be a good place for you to have your conversation?
  • Where would your participant feel most comfortable?
 A project room on campus has been chosen as the place for the interview. Given our participants are students, this should be convenient and familiar for them.
4. Energy
  • How would you like your interviewee to feel during and after the interview?
  • Would you like them to feel calm and safe?
  • Would you like them to feel excited? Inspired?
 Relaxed and safe, as if they're talking with a friend.
5. Medium
  • What tools, artifacts or visuals might help prompt the interviewee's thinking?
  • Do you need your interviewee to engage with something tangible?
 Jason: Maybe some pictures of food or something? Do you guys have any idea for this?

Your checklist List your questions Rationale
  • Explain who you are and what your research is about.
  • Remind your participant how long the interview will take.
  • Tell your participant their answers are anonymous.
  • Explain who you are and what your research is about
  • Explain the role of the note taker.
  • Ask your participant to be honest and open with you -that's exactly what you need and there are no wrong or right answers,and they won't offend you in any way.
  • Explain that you might be asking 'obvious' questions and that you'll be asking 'why' a lot- you don't want to be led by your own assumptions.
  • Ask if they've got any questions before you begin the interview.
  • Can you please describe to me your meals last week?
  • How often do you eat at home? Which meals?
  • Do you typically eat by yourself, or with others? Why?
  • (if eat with others) Do they have any dietary requirements different to yours? (eg vegan, allergy, diet?)
 How and when people eat meals
  • On a typical week, how many meals would you buy "ready to eat"? This could include restaurants, take-away, etc?
  • How often do you usually buy food?
  • What kind of food do you usually buy and why? (maybe they buy fresh food and we can discuss food wastage.)
  • What factors affect the food you buy? (maybe they concern about the shelf life and will tell us how they try to reduce food wastage..)
  • Do you plan your meals before shopping? Why/why not?
  • Do you do your grocery shopping in store (instead of online)? Why?
  • Do you make a shopping list before buying food? Why
  • Do you ever buy bulk foods? How often? Why?
  • What type of food do you like to cook?
 How people buy food
  • Do you ever have to throw food out? How often? Why? (this question is particularly important, so make sure to ask follow up questions based on their response)
  • What do you do with food you don't eat? (regular garbage, green bin, compost, dog?)
  • Have you ever taken any action to reduce the amount you throw out?
 Factors leading to food waste (cooking)
  • Do you ever have take away meals?
  • When you do, was it planned in advance? (before that day)
  • Are you ever surprised by the portion sizes? (larger than expected, smaller than expected, both?)
  • Are there ever any leftovers? What do you do with them? (eat them the next day? within a week? never?)
