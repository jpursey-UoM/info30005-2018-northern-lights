Interview Guide

This table shows our questions for the initial interview stage. Together, these questions will provide us with a deeper understanding of how people cook in their homes, the factors leading to food wastage, how people order food, and they role technology plays in their kitchens.

Setting the right foundation
  • What is the context you're focusing on?
  • Who is involved and why?
  • Why do you need to conduct an interview?
 The food wastage caused by individuals.
Every individual who cook and lives a busy life. Increasing life pace leaves little time for them to cook, making food wastage more likely to occur.
  • What is the desired outcome of this interview?
  • What would you like to learn or validate?
 Confirm that people waste food at home.
Find out the reasons for household food wastage.
  • Where would be a good place for you to have your conversation?
  • Where would your participant feel most comfortable?
 Project room has been chosen to be the place for the interview.

  • Would you like them to feel calm and safe?

 Relaxed and safe.

 How and why people cook
Remind your participant how long the interview will take.
Tell your participant their answers are anonymous.
  • How and how often do usually you buy food? (go grocery, home delivery meal kit?)
  • Is food wastage a problem for you? If so, how does it happen?
  • Do you ever have to throw food out? Why?
  • Have you ever taken any action to reduce the amount you throw out?
  • Do you buy bulk foods a lot?
  • What type of food do you like to cook?
  • Do you make a plan before going to supermarket?
 Factors causing food wastage
Explain who you are and what your research is about
Explain the role of the note taker.
  • Do you ever share food with your friends and family? (eg bringing leftovers when you/they visit)
  • Do you ever order meals online? How often? Which services?
  • When do you usually order food? Workdays or Weekend?
  • What is it you like most about ordering meals online?
  • When you order food online, was it planned? How far in advance?
  • What factors affect your decision to order meals?
  • Are there any situations in which you always order online?
  • Do you have any concerns about ordering meals online?
  • What factor would help you to decide the place you order meal from?
How people order food
Ask your participant to be honest and open with you -that's exactly what you need and there are no wrong or right answers,and they won't offend you in any way.
Explain that you might be asking 'obvious' questions and that you'll be asking 'why' a lot- you don't want to be led by your own assumptions.
  • Can you remember a specific time you ordered a meal online? Perhaps the most recent or memorable time?
  • Which day was it? What time?
  • What did you do before/after eating?
  • Where you with anyone else? Who?
  • Which service did you use? Why?
  • What did you order?
  • How much did it cost, roughly?
  • How did you feel about the ordering process? Anything in particular that you loved/hated?
  • How did you feel about the delivery?
  • How did you feel about the food itself?
  • Is there anything you would change about the experience?
 Specific instance to understand more deeply how and why the participant orders meals online
